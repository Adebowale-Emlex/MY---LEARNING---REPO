About Me

I am focused on building and studying local-first civil infrastructure and digital systems, with a strong interest in how engineering, technology, and governance intersect in real-world development.

Trained in civil engineering, I think primarily in systems; physical systems like roads, water, and structures, and digital systems that support planning, transparency, coordination, and long-term maintenance. I use digital systems as tools to improve how infrastructure is designed, delivered, monitored, and governed, not as ends in themselves.

Alongside my technical interests, I have taken on leadership roles and responsibilities that shaped how I think about governance, decision-making, and collective action. These experiences reinforced my belief that infrastructure success depends as much on institutions, incentives, and people as it does on technical design.

My work and learning are guided by a local-first approach, starting from local context, capacity, and needs, while engaging global tools, ideas, and standards where they add value. I am particularly interested in systems that promote transparency, accountability, and long-term stewardship of public and community infrastructure.

I am continuously learning, building, and experimenting at the intersection of civil infrastructure, digital systems, governance, and leadership, with the goal of contributing to more resilient, trustworthy, and well-managed infrastructure systems.
